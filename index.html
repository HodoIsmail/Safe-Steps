Key Principles

The 6 principles of safeguarding are essential because everyone has the right to live free from abuse and neglect. These principles help achieve that by

  • Empowering vulnerable individuals to make their own decisions
  • Preventing harm early on
  • Responding to risks in a respectful, minimally invasive way
  • Ensuring adequate knowledge and training to prevent abuse
  • Partnering with organizations and communities to support the vulnerable
  • Clarifying safeguarding responsibilities for everyone involved
By following these principles, we can help prevent abuse and provide the highest level of care to protect vulnerable people.

Adult Safeguarding

Adult safeguarding is crucial because it protects vulnerable individuals from abuse, neglect, and exploitation, ensuring their right to live safely and with dignity. Adults who are most at risk typically include the elderly, those with physical or cognitive disabilities, individuals with mental health challenges, and those experiencing financial instability or social isolation. These groups often face greater susceptibility to various forms of harm, including emotional, physical, and financial abuse. By prioritizing adult safeguarding, society promotes not only the well-being of these individuals but also upholds the values of respect and care within our communities.

Child Safeguarding

Child safeguarding is essential as it ensures the protection and well-being of children, shielding them from abuse, neglect, and exploitation. Vulnerable children—such as those from disadvantaged backgrounds, those with disabilities, and those experiencing family instability or violence—are often at heightened risk. Safeguarding measures help create safe environments where children can thrive, learn, and develop without fear of harm. By prioritizing child safeguarding, society not only protects its most vulnerable members but also fosters a foundation for a healthier, more secure future for everyone.
